Event description

What is it?



ShipIT Day is Accesa’s 24-hour event in which you and your team can become a ninja expert in delivering innovative ideas.


What qualifies as a ShipIT idea? For this year’s edition - “ShipIT to Japan“, we aim to bring out the best ideas related to Digital Workplace projects, meaning technologies and ways of working that allow people to connect, collaborate, communicate and co-operate by using technology (without necessarily being together face to face). People’s productivity, engagement and working health sit at the very core of a complex digital workplace ecosystem. Transforming the traditional workplace into a digital one involves reinventing the working environment and breaking down the barriers between people, workplaces and technologies, and empowering their employees to be productive and creative wherever they are. As this area has known a fast development, several more innovations are already on their way: Google glass, iWatch, connected objects, electronic pens, 3D printing, Wi-Fi printing, curved screens and many more. Clearly, there is no end to innovation, so bring out the samurai in you!

How does it work?



  1. Submit your project idea by uploading it online, in the “Ideas” section, at shipit.accesa.eu .
  2. Project ideas are then published within the organization through internal communication channels.
  3. Project owners then have to find teammates to work on their project (tip: create diversity in your team by bringing in BA’s, QA’s… ). At the same time, employees can also take initiative and ask project owners to allow them to be part of the team.
  4. After the kick-off, you and your team have 24 hours to implement the idea and pitch it in 10’.
  5. Enjoy delicious Japanese food & refreshing drinks to get your mana.
  6. Pitch your idea in 10’.
  7. After the allocated 24 hours, the ideas are evaluated by a committee and the winning team is selected
  8. Winners are awarded and then everyone is invited to an after-event celebration.

Concept

Why the Japan-inspired ShipIT event?


24 hours to craft a great digital workplace-related idea might not seem enough so find inspiration in Japan’s most known state of mind: wabi-sabi & enso.


Zen practitioners relate the idea of the open circle to wabi-sabi, the beauty of imperfection. In ShipIT we’ll focus on the ideas & value of the projects, as no one expects finished, bugless projects at the end of the event.


What is Enso? A Japanese circle that is hand-drawn through one or two uninhibited brushstrokes to express a moment when the mind is free to let he body create. The circle may be open or closed. In the former case, the circle is incomplete, allowing for movement and development as well as the perfection of all things.
